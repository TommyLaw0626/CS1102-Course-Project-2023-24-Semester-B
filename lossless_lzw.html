Lempel Ziv Welch Encoding

  • Since codes consume less space than strings, LZW works by transforming strings into codes to get the image compressed. LZW relies on the dictionary that is built to encounter the patterns in the data.
  • Read the symbols’ pattern/sequence → group symbols to strings → convert strings to codes.

    • Concept: Let’s consider an 8-bit grayscale image which contains 256 different intensities levels.

    More example:
    Output: 39 39 126 126 256 258 260 259 257 126
    Total no. of bits (after compression) = 10 * 9 = 90
    Total no. of bits (before compression) = 16 * 8 = 128
    90<128

    Some combinations of Algorithms:
    Adaptive dictionary algorithms:

    • Examples: LZ77 and LZ78 compression methods, including LZW.
    • These algorithms are dynamic and are meant to update the dictionary of patterns in the data. They operate similarly to LZW as they replace the repeated patterns with shorter codes.
    • Use for PNG and ZIP

    Flate/Deflation:

    • Combining LZ77 and Huffman coding to maximize the output compression.
    • Flate will first substitute the repeated patterns with shorter codes with LZ77 methods before it utilizes Huffman coding to further compress the output.
    • Use for PNG and PDF
