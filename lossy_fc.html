Fractal Compression

Fractal Compression is a technique that seeks to construct an approximation of the original image that is precise enough to be acceptable, closer to the original at higher compression ratios.

  • Idea of Affine transformation is applied: any combination of a rotation, scale change, skew or translation (can be done with matrix)

    • A form matric of affine transformations

    Techniques:

      1. Iterated Function Systems (IFS): The fundamental mathematical framework which forms basis for iterating structures, which is a crucial component in most fractal compression algorithms.
      2. Range Blocks and Domain Blocks: The concept of dividing the image into range blocks and finding matching domain blocks which allows efficient representation of similarities which helps compression process.
      3. Quadtree Compression: A hierarchical technique that efficiently represents images by dividing them into quadrants, which is combined often with fractal compression to capture local and global similarities in the image.
      4. Similarity Measures: A technique that quantifies the similarty between range and domain blocks.
        • SSIM
        • NCC
      5. Encoding and Decoding: Algorithms that accurately represent and reconstruct the image from fractal codes, which the efficiency and effectiveness vary due to the type of implementation.
      6. Domain Pool: A technique that enhances compression efficiency bt reusing previously encoded domain blocks, but the size and management of the domain pool affects compression performance.
      7. Pre-processing Techniques: Various techniques including colour space transformations, filtering, histogram equalizations and etc., which reduce noise and improve compression results.
    Fractal Compression also uses some technical algorithms:
  • Partical Swarm Optimization (PSO)
  • Genetic Algorithm
  • Crowding Optimization Method
  • Scatter Search
